Cell Morphology Tracker

Project Type: MATLAB

Purpose

At Seattle Children's Research Institute, our lab aimed to further characterize the mast cell surface proteome to obtain a better understanding of how mast cells function in health and disease. Cell surface protein CD98 heavy chain (CD98hc), encoded by the Slc3a2 gene, was identified as a key protein. Slc3a2 gene disruption was identified to result in a significant reduction in CD98hc expression, adhesion, and proliferation. To better characterize SLC3a2 gene disruption, it was imperative to develop a method to track cell phenotypes.

A walkthrough

I used MATLAB to create a program that uses image processing and computer vision to analyze biological samples on a microscope slide, determining cell morphology differences between wild-type cells and mutant (SLC3a2 deficient) cells.
